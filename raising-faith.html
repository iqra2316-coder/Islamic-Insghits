Lady Fatima (as) and Her Role as a Mother
Lady Fatima al-Zahra (as) raised two of the greatest men in Islamic history — Imam Hassan (as) and Imam Hussain (as). Her approach to parenting was rooted in deep love, constant prayer, Quranic education, and moral example. She spent hours in prayer while her children slept, teaching them by her own example that the relationship with Allah comes before everything else. For Muslim parents today, her model offers timeless, practical wisdom. She never raised her voice in anger at her children — yet raised two Imams who would change the course of history.

Building an Islamic Home — Where Faith Begins
The home is the most powerful educational institution a child will ever attend. Research consistently shows that the values, habits, and character formed in the first seven years of life are the most enduring. Islam recognized this fourteen centuries ago — prescribing specific practices for the Islamic home: beginning each day with Bismillah, praying together as a family, reciting Quran at night, limiting exposure to harmful content, and filling the home with the dhikr of Allah. This article provides a practical, step-by-step guide to building a genuinely Islamic home environment.

Teaching Children to Love the Quran
The Quran is the greatest gift we can give our children — but love for the Quran must be cultivated, not forced. Many Muslim parents make the mistake of introducing the Quran to their children through punishment and pressure, creating a lifelong association of the Holy Book with fear and obligation. The Prophet ﷺ and the AhlulBayt show us a different way: through beautiful recitation, stories, games, rewards, and making Quran time the most special and joyful part of the child's day. This article shares proven, faith-informed strategies for raising Quran-loving children.
