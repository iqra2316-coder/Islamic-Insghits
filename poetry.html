✍ Poetry · Karbala
From Cemetery to a Holy Sanctuary — Jannat al-Baqi
Islamic Insights · Poetry Series
يَا بَاقِيَ الْبَاقِيَاتِ الصَّالِحَاتِ
A piece of land not like any other,
Carrying saints unlike any other,
Beneath its earth sleep the beloved ones,
The family of the Prophet — daughters and sons.

Where Fatima's heart was buried in grief,
Where Imams rest, beyond belief,
Where the companions of the chosen one lie,
Under Madinah's eternal sky.

They destroyed your domes, they broke your stones,
But they could never silence the groans,
Of millions who weep, who visit, who pray,
At Jannat al-Baqi, till the judgement day.

O land of the sacred, O garden of peace,
In your soil all sorrow must cease,
For those who rest within your embrace,
Are alive — with Allah — in the highest place.
✍ Poetry · AhlulBayt
Ya Hussain — The Eternal Call
Islamic Insights · AhlulBayt Series
لَبَّيْكَ يَا حُسَيْن
On the plains of Karbala you stood,
Alone but never misunderstood,
With seventy-two hearts beating as one,
You faced the darkness, you faced the gun.

They cut the water, they blocked the way,
But thirst could not make your spirit sway,
You said: disgrace is far from us,
And gave your life without a fuss.

O grandson of the Prophet ﷺ most dear,
Your sacrifice grows louder every year,
The more they tried to bury your name,
The higher rose your undying flame.

Labbayk ya Hussain — here we stand,
Carrying your message across every land,
That truth must rise and falsehood must fall,
Till the end of time — ya Hussain, your call.
✍ Poetry · Prophet ﷺ
Mercy to the Worlds — A Tribute to the Prophet ﷺ
Islamic Insights · Prophetic Series
وَمَا أَرْسَلْنَاكَ إِلَّا رَحْمَةً لِّلْعَالَمِينَ
Before you came, the world was blind,
Lost in darkness of heart and mind,
Then Allah sent His mercy rare,
Muhammad ﷺ — light beyond compare.

You fed the hungry, freed the slave,
You gave the hopeless what they craved,
You wiped the tears of those in grief,
You were the world's eternal relief.

You held no grudge, you bore no hate,
Even for those who sealed your fate,
You prayed for those who threw the stones,
And wept for those who broke their bones.

O Prophet ﷺ of the final hour,
Our hearts still bloom from your gentle flower,
No matter how the centuries turn,
For you alone our spirits yearn.
✍ Poetry · Imam Ali (as)
The Lion of Allah — Imam Ali (as)
Islamic Insights · AhlulBayt Series
أَنَا مَدِينَةُ الْعِلْمِ وَعَلِيٌّ بَابُهَا
In the house of Allah you first prayed,
Before the world had heard the faith displayed,
A child of ten, yet faith of mountains tall,
The first to answer the Prophet's call.

On Laylat al-Mabit you slept his bed,
Offering your life instead,
No man has loved with love so pure,
No heart has been so strong and sure.

Gate of knowledge, sword of truth,
Lion of Allah in age and youth,
Your justice filled the farthest land,
From the Euphrates to the sand.

They struck you down at dawn in prayer,
But Ali's light is everywhere,
In every word of truth we speak,
It is your face, O Ali, we seek.
✍ Poetry · Spiritual
The Wandering Heart — Seeking Allah
Islamic Insights · Spirituality Series
My heart is like a ship at sea,
Tossed by the waves of what I see,
The dunya calls with golden voice,
But You, O Allah, are my only choice.

I've chased the shadows of this world,
Watched every dream become unfurled,
And found that nothing fills the space,
That only You can truly grace.

So take this heart that wanders far,
And guide it like a wandering star,
Back to your door, back to your light,
Back to the dawn that ends my night.

For in your remembrance, souls find rest,
In your worship, hearts are truly blessed,
There is no peace, there is no shore,
Except the peace of knowing You more.
✍ Poetry · Lady Fatima (as)
Flower of the Prophet — Lady Fatima al-Zahra (as)
Islamic Insights · AhlulBayt Series
فَاطِمَةُ بَضْعَةٌ مِنِّي
"Fatima is a part of me," he said ﷺ,
And in those words a universe was spread,
To hurt her is to wound the Prophet's heart,
Her joy his joy — from the very start.

She ground the mill until her hands were worn,
She wore the robe of patience, prayer-torn,
Yet asked for nothing but her father's face,
And in Allah's nearness found her grace.

She wept for Islam's coming years,
And watered faith with holy tears,
She raised two sons who changed the earth,
And gave a martyred message birth.

O Zahra, flower of the Prophet's tree,
Your fragrance fills eternity,
And those who love you, love him too,
And Allah's mercy falls on you.
