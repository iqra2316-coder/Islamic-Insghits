The Eternal Stand of Imam Hussain (as) at Karbala
Islamic Insights Team10 Muharram 61 AH👁 21,500 views

On the 10th of Muharram, 61 AH — known forever as the Day of Ashura — a pivotal moment in Islamic history unfolded on the scorching plains of Karbala in present-day Iraq. Imam Hussain ibn Ali (as), the grandson of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, the son of Imam Ali (as) and Lady Fatima al-Zahra (as), stood against the forces of tyranny and injustice with a small group of 72 loyal companions and family members.

يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ إِنَّ رَسُولَ اللهِ قَالَ: مَنْ رَأَى سُلْطَاناً جَائِراً… فَلَمْ يُغَيِّرْ عَلَيْهِ بِفِعْلٍ وَلَا قَوْلٍ كَانَ حَقّاً عَلَى اللهِ أَنْ يُدْخِلَهُ مَدْخَلَهُ

Background: The Call from Kufa

After the death of Muawiyah ibn Abi Sufyan, his son Yazid — a man known for his immoral conduct and open violations of Islamic law — assumed the caliphate. The people of Kufa, Iraq, sent thousands of letters to Imam Hussain (as) in Makkah, pledging their support and urging him to come lead them. The Imam sent his cousin Muslim ibn Aqeel to Kufa to verify the sincerity of this support. Muslim initially received overwhelming backing, with over 18,000 people pledging allegiance.

The Journey to Karbala

Imam Hussain (as) departed from Makkah on the 8th of Dhul Hijjah, refusing to desecrate the sanctity of the Holy Kaaba by allowing conflict there. He traveled with his family, his companions, and the women and children of the Hashimite clan. Along the way, he learned that the Kufan governor Ubaydullah ibn Ziyad had arrested and executed Muslim ibn Aqeel, and that the people of Kufa had abandoned their pledge.

"I have not risen to cause mischief, but to seek reform in the Ummah of my grandfather. I wish to bid good and forbid evil, and follow the conduct of my grandfather and my father Ali ibn Abi Talib." — Imam Hussain (as)

The Imam continued nevertheless, knowing what lay ahead. On the 2nd of Muharram 61 AH, the caravan reached the land of Karbala, where they were stopped and encircled by a massive army under the command of Umar ibn Sa'd, acting on orders from Yazid and Ibn Ziyad.

The Days of Muharram

For the next eight days, the army of Yazid surrounded the camp of Imam Hussain (as). On the 7th of Muharram, access to the Euphrates river was cut off, depriving the Imam's camp of water — including the women and small children, including the six-month-old infant Ali Asghar. Despite this, the Imam and his companions remained steadfast in their resolve.

On the eve of Ashura, the Imam gathered his companions and family and released them from any obligation, telling them the enemy sought only him. Every single one refused to leave, declaring their willingness to sacrifice their lives for the truth. Abbas ibn Ali (as), the Imam's brother, gathered water for the camp under enemy fire, ultimately losing both his arms and his life in the attempt.

The Day of Ashura — 10 Muharram

On the morning of the 10th of Muharram, after the Fajr prayer, Imam Hussain (as) rode out and addressed the army of Yazid, presenting clear Islamic arguments against their actions. One by one, his companions and family members fought and were martyred. Imam Hussain (as) himself was the last to fall — his body bearing 72 wounds — as he performed his final prostration to Allah.

"هَيْهَاتَ مِنَّا الذِّلَّةُ" — "Disgrace is far from us!" — These words, echoing across the desert of Karbala, became the eternal motto of all who resist oppression.

The Aftermath and the Message of Karbala

After the martyrdom of Imam Hussain (as) and his companions, the women and children — including Imam Ali ibn Hussain (Zainul Abideen, as) who was ill — were taken prisoner to Kufa and then Damascus. Lady Zainab bint Ali (as), the daughter of Imam Ali (as) and Lady Fatima (as), delivered powerful speeches in the courts of Ibn Ziyad and Yazid himself, exposing the injustice of the massacre and ensuring the message of Karbala reached every corner of the Muslim world.

The sacrifice of Imam Hussain (as) at Karbala was not merely a historical event — it was the preservation of the soul of Islam. His stand against tyranny, his refusal to pledge allegiance to oppression, and his ultimate sacrifice sent a message that echoes through every generation: that truth must never bow to falsehood, and justice must never be sacrificed at the altar of power.
