Abdullah Shah Ghazi Shrine Attacked in Karachi
The revered shrine of Abdullah Shah Ghazi, one of the most visited religious sites in Karachi, Pakistan, was targeted in a violent attack that shocked the Muslim community. Abdullah Shah Ghazi is considered a great Islamic saint and scholar whose tomb has drawn pilgrims for centuries. The attack, condemned by religious leaders across Pakistan, has renewed calls for better security at sacred sites and renewed discussions about the rising tide of extremism targeting places of Islamic heritage and devotion.

News · World
Millions Gather for Arbaeen — World's Largest Peaceful March
Over 20 million pilgrims from across the globe converged on the holy city of Karbala, Iraq, for the annual Arbaeen commemoration — marking 40 days after the martyrdom of Imam Hussain (as). The Arbaeen walk, during which millions trek on foot to reach Karbala, is recognized as the largest annual human gathering on earth. Pilgrims from more than 40 countries participate, with Iraqi families hosting millions of visitors entirely free of charge in one of the most remarkable displays of Islamic brotherhood and hospitality in the world.

UN Report Highlights Rising Islamophobia Worldwide
A new United Nations report has documented a sharp global rise in incidents of Islamophobia, hate crimes, and discrimination against Muslim communities across Europe, North America, and parts of Asia. The report calls on governments to enact stronger protections for religious minorities, improve hate crime reporting systems, and challenge the systemic biases that enable Islamophobia to persist in media, politics, and public discourse. Muslim civil society organizations have welcomed the report while calling for concrete action beyond rhetoric.
