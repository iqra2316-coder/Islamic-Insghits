Lady Khadija al-Kubra — The Esteemed Wife of the Prophet ﷺ
Lady Khadija al-Kubra (as) was a woman of extraordinary character, wisdom, and unshakeable faith. She was the first person to embrace Islam and the greatest supporter of the Prophet ﷺ. Born into the noble clan of Banu Asad of the Quraysh tribe, she was already renowned for her intelligence and business acumen before her marriage to the Prophet ﷺ. Her wealth, sacrifice, and undying love formed the very foundation upon which the early Islamic mission was built. The Prophet ﷺ often described her as the best of women of her time.

The Night of Hijra — The Prophet's ﷺ Migration to Medina
On the night that would change history forever, the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ left his beloved city of Makkah accompanied by his closest companion Abu Bakr (ra). The Quraysh had plotted to assassinate the Prophet ﷺ, surrounding his house with swords. But Allah's protection prevailed, and the Prophet ﷺ walked past his would-be assassins unseen. This migration — the Hijra — marks Year 1 of the Islamic calendar and the beginning of the first Islamic state in Madinah al-Munawwarah.

Jannat al-Baqi — From Cemetery to Holy Sanctuary
Jannat al-Baqi is the ancient cemetery of Madinah al-Munawwarah, located just beside Masjid an-Nabawi. It holds the graves of some of the most beloved figures in Islamic history — including four of the twelve Imams of the AhlulBayt, the wives and children of the Prophet ﷺ, and thousands of his companions. A piece of land not like any other, Jannat al-Baqi carries a spiritual weight that draws millions of pilgrims who come to offer salutations and prayers for its sacred inhabitants.

How Did Islam Spread: By Sword or By Sincere Conviction?
One of the most persistent misconceptions about Islam is that it spread primarily through forced conversion at the point of a sword. A careful study of history, however, reveals a far more nuanced and inspiring picture. The rapid spread of Islam across Arabia, Persia, Africa, and beyond was driven primarily by the compelling moral example of Muslims, the justice of Islamic governance, and the clarity and beauty of the Quranic message — a message that resonated deeply with peoples long oppressed by unjust systems.

Jesus Christ (Prophet Isa) and His Fight Against Oppression
In the Islamic tradition, Prophet Isa ibn Maryam (Jesus, son of Mary) is one of the five greatest Prophets (Ulul Azm). He was born miraculously to the Virgin Mary, performed extraordinary miracles by the permission of Allah, and was raised to the heavens before his crucifixion. His message — like that of all the Prophets — was one of pure monotheism, justice, and compassion for the poor and oppressed. Muslims await his return as a sign of the Last Hour.

The Golden Age of Islam — When Muslims Led the World
From the 8th to the 14th century, the Islamic civilization experienced an extraordinary Golden Age. Muslim scholars led the world in mathematics, astronomy, medicine, philosophy, architecture, and literature. Figures like Ibn Sina (Avicenna), Al-Khawarizmi, Ibn Rushd, and Al-Biruni laid the intellectual foundations that would later fuel the European Renaissance. This rich legacy reminds Muslims of the transformative power of combining faith with the pursuit of knowledge.
